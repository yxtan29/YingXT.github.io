Form and Color

Pop-Up Structure

Challenge

Experiment with paper and explore the world of pop-ups to create interesting two and three-dimensional form from simple media. The pop-up must be one that contains letters, two colors of paper, or an image.

Solution

For my pop-up, I decided to make one using two colors of paper, which consisted of white bristol and black construction paper. I also decided to add on two other elements with two more kinds of paper, transparency film for creating a see-through component, and newsprint for added texture. As this is an exploration of creating structure with limited or simple materials, I used only glue, tape, scissors, and one marker to create the pop-up books and structure. The black pop-up structure is made with black construction paper, which is cut into rectangles into each spine of where the paper is folded to create multilayered steps, columns, and terraces, and pushed in/out in an alternating sequence. Lastly, I added a little figure sitting inside the create the illusion of a huge, looming, wall structure surrounding it, when in reality the pop-up structure is only the size of a book.
